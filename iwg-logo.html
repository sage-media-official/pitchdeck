Six typographic logo directions for a luxury watch house — buying, selling and curating fine timepieces. Pure type, no gimmicks: the letters themselves must feel like a well-finished dial. Each direction is shown on ivory and on charcoal with champagne gold.

Why it works: the W becomes an O-cased dial holding hands at 10:10 — the position every watch is photographed in. It's still a three-letter typographic mark (I·O·G reads as the case around IWG), but collectors will smile the moment they see it.

Best for: the brand's "hidden detail" — watch pouches, buckle engraving, loading animation on the site.

Our recommendation

Lead with Direction 01 (The Didone House) as the primary wordmark — it carries the most luxury authority per square inch — and pair it with Direction 02 (The Dial Monogram) as the compact mark for avatars, seals and packaging. The two share the same champagne-gold-on-charcoal system and cover every size from a hoarding to a WhatsApp DP. Direction 06 is the collector's easter egg if the brand wants one.
